PROJECT BRIEF

Redefining the customer experience (physical &/or online) for a gourmet/ fine dining restaurant. 

PROBLEM STATEMENT

People in their 30s and 40s face challenges in dating due to ageist judgments, a lack of available, safe offline dating spaces, and the discomfort of first dates, resulting in unmet desires for meaningful connections.

DESIGN PROCESS

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

Initially, we conducted an preliminary literature review with the aim of mapping out customer problems in restaurants, from which we derived multiple problem statements and subsequently applied why and how laddering techniques to identify the root cause of the problem and its impact. This enabled us to construct our Solution Devoid Problem Statement (SDPS). Following this, to validate our previous findings and gain deeper insights into the problem, we conducted interviews with five stakeholders, resulting in the refinement of our final SDPS.

Based on the insights, a set of stakeholder requirements were created, prioritised, and tested.

IDEATION

After gathering our insights, we defined our user requirements and used them as the foundation for brainstorming solutions. These ideas were then transformed into two distinct concepts. The two concepts were evaluated using a morphological (requirement-concept) matrix, and Concept 2 was chosen as a consequence.

TESTING+LEARNING

We experimented with the voice, speed, tone and pauses. we also created a system persona to reflect the personality of the bot. This was then tested with a small group of people and altered based on feedback.

PROPOSED SOLUTION IMPLEMENTATION

Additionally, we engaged in multiple brainstorming sessions to strategize the training and testing data that could be integrated into the bot's development.

PROTOTYPE

A conversational user interface that enhances the dating and customer experiences by facilitating natural conversations and improving customer service at dating-specific restaurants

GUIDEBOOK

STORYBOARD

LEARNINGS

  • Humans are social beings: Through our experiences, we've learned to appreciate the challenges of dating, and this has deepened our empathy for the importance of socializing to combat loneliness.
  • Understanding machine Learning: Developing this app has provided us with valuable insights into the feasibility and technical constraints of machine learning. It has given us a clearer understanding of how machine learning functions.
  • Continuous testing for design: We've discovered that conducting consistent testing throughout the conversational UX development process, rather than solely at the end with prototypes, significantly enhances the overall design intervention experience.

FUTURE WORK

The intervention gave us insights into what its strengths were, while also shedding light on the gaps that still existed in the intervention as well as in the ecosystem. some areas for suture wrk that we identified were -

  • Perform another round of usability testing with high fidelity prototypes to get feedback on the bot’s interactions
  • Brainstorming ideas for additional fun interactions using the bot
  • Improving communication skills (to be less intrusive, understanding social cues, etc.)
  • Bridging the language barrier gap
