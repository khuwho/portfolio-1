PROBLEM STATEMENT

Inadequate/ineffective therapy caused by a general lack of alignment between the therapeutic approach and the individual's specific needs and preferences can result in unsuccessful therapy, prolonging the duration and severity of mental illness in patients.

DESIGN PROCESS

DISCOVERING THE PROBLEM

First, a brief preliminary literature review was conducted in order to develop a stakeholder archetype and an exploratory journey map. A habitat was chosen from the journey map. Then, a habitat study and additional literature review were carried out in order to draft a few problem statements. One of these problem statements was chosen based on its severity, urgency, impact, frequency, and personal choice. A why and how laddering was conducted on this statement to arrive at a draft Solution Devoid Problem Statement (SDPS) (mentioned above)

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM (TASK CLARIFICATION)

To validate my hypothesis, I conducted additional research on the selected problem area. Through an extensive literature review, market analysis, stakeholder interviews, expert interview, and survey, I narrowed down the factors affecting the success of therapy for a person with mental health issues to factors such as: therapist-patient mismatch, the absence of a structured treatment plan, patient adherence to advice, and the challenge of monitoring progress.

Based on the insights, a set of stakeholder requirements were created, prioritised, and tested.

CONCEPTUALISATION

These requirements were then used to brainstorm ideas, these ideas were organised into idea buckets. The idea buckets were then developed into concepts through a morphological matrix, resulting in three concepts. These concepts were evaluated against the established requirements and concept 1 was selected based on the results.

FINAL CONCEPT

A comprehensive digital platform (smart phone app) to democratise access to high-quality mental health resources and support, bridging gaps in care and fostering well-being for all. Some features of the platform include: free resources from mental health, optimises the patient-therapist match, as well as provides tools for monitoring mental well-being.

A detailed description, along with a visual representation of the final concept, was presented for stakeholder validation. Their feedback was carefully assessed, and appropriate changes were incorporated into the development of the concept.

DESIGN INTERVENTION

Following this, in order to finalise how the design intervention would be implemented, a user case scenario that included every element of the final concept was generated. Mockups of the final design intervention were created along with a detailed storyboard. These were used to perform another round of stakeholder validation .

1

Having a professional therapist match you to a therapist that specialises in your mental health issues

Obtaining assistance from a professional therapist to connect you with a mental health specialist who has expertise in addressing your specific mental health challenges to prevent mismatches between therapists and patients

2

Free Self CBT Templates designed by therapists to help people who can’t afford therapy

Self-CBT worksheets or templates are resources that people can use on their own to exercise CBT procedures and process their feelings, thoughts, and actions.

3

Build a community.

Join the conversation and enhance your understanding of mental health with therapist-led, free events, including support groups led by psychologists, journaling, and more

4

Tracking your progress along with your therapist

Obtaining assistance from a professional therapist to connect you with a mental health specialist who has expertise in addressing your specific mental health challenges to prevent mismatches between therapists and patients

TESTING + IMPROVEMENTS

Testing took place at multiple stages during the project's development. It began by testing the requirements with a therapist, followed by forming a stakeholder review plan and evaluating the conceptual design. The conceptual design underwent iterative improvements based on feedback. Subsequently, the mockups and final storyboards were created. The embodiment design was further refined based on feedback, ultimately leading to the final design intervention.

Some learnings were:

  • Online communities should be monitored by a therapist to ensure that users aren't oversharing information that might trigger others.
  • Time management tools and other extra features were removed since they appeared to overwhelm consumers.
  • Articles on mental health were frequently neglected by users, therefore they were replaced with story- or status-like features where information could be presented in an easy-to-read way. More people interacted with these articles in the short story format.

LEARNINGS

  • Through this project, I was better able to understand the complexity of mental health issues.
  • During my involvement in this project, I gained the practical knowledge and skills needed to effectively collect, organize, and visualise data for extracting profound insights.

FUTURE WORK
